Models

There are countless ways to value stocks. Below are three common ways to value publicly traded stocks.

Enterprise Discounted Cash Flow

This model values a company by estimating its future cash flows and discounting them to their present value. It takes into account the entire enterprise, including debt and equity, and can be used to evaluate companies with significant debt.

Discounted Dividend Model

This model values a company based on the present value of its future dividend payments. It assumes that the company will continue to pay dividends at the same rate, and discounts them back to their present value. It is commonly used for evaluating mature, dividend-paying companies.

Buffett Formula

Buffett's Intrinsic Value Formula is a simple formula used to estimate the true worth of a company based on its fundamentals.
